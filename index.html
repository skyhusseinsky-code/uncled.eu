What we cover

Six Interconnected Domains

These six fields don't exist in isolation. Cybersecurity requires mathematics. Ethical hacking requires computer science. Computer science is built on electronics. Everything is formalized through informatics. Here, we embrace those deep connections and explore them together.

🛡️
01 — Core Domain
Cybersecurity
The practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. Cybersecurity is not a product — it is a discipline. Understanding it requires knowing how systems are built before you can understand how they break. We cover both defensive and offensive perspectives, from security principles and threat modeling to real attack techniques and incident response.
CIA TriadThreat ModelingNetwork SecurityCryptographyMalware AnalysisIncident Response

02 — Core Domain
Electronics
The physical substrate beneath every digital system. Before you can understand a computer, you need to understand what a transistor does. Electronics bridges the gap between physics and computation — from Ohm's Law to microcontrollers to embedded systems. Knowing electronics helps you understand hardware vulnerabilities, IoT security, and why certain optimizations are made at the silicon level.
Ohm's LawTransistorsLogic GatesArduinoRaspberry PiEmbedded Systems

03 — Core Domain
Informatica
The science of information — how data is represented, stored, transmitted, and processed. Informatics provides the formal framework for everything else. It includes networking protocols, database design, operating system concepts, and the theory of computation. Without a solid informatics foundation, you are guessing rather than understanding what happens inside a machine.
TCP/IPDatabasesOperating SystemsFile SystemsProtocolsData Modeling
↗
 ∑
04 — Core Domain
Wiskunde / Mathematics
The language that all of computing is written in. Boolean algebra gave us logic gates. Number theory gave us RSA encryption. Graph theory gives us network routing algorithms. Discrete mathematics underpins data structures. Linear algebra powers machine learning. You cannot truly understand cryptography, algorithms, or security without the mathematical foundations — and they are more accessible than most people think.
Boolean AlgebraNumber TheoryGraph TheoryProbabilityDiscrete MathLinear Algebra

 ⚙️
05 — Core Domain
Computer Science
The theoretical and practical study of computation. Computer science explains why algorithms perform the way they do, how memory is managed, how operating systems schedule processes, and how compilers turn human-readable code into machine instructions. This is the foundation that separates programmers who copy code from engineers who understand it — and security researchers who truly know their target.
AlgorithmsData StructuresBig-OOS DesignCompilersMemory Management
↗

06 — Core Domain
Ethical Hacking
Using the attacker's mindset to find and fix vulnerabilities before malicious actors do. Ethical hacking — also called penetration testing or red teaming — is a profession and a methodology. It draws on every other domain: you need networking, programming, operating system knowledge, and creative thinking. Here we cover the methodology, the tools, the techniques, and — critically — the legal and ethical framework.
Pen TestingCTF ChallengesOSINTWeb ExploitationReverse EngineeringSocial Engineering

About CyberLab

Everything is Connected

CyberLab is a personal knowledge platform built by Hussein — a student with a deep obsession for understanding how technology actually works, not just how to use it. The goal from day one was to create a resource that treats readers as intelligent people who want real explanations, not dumbed-down summaries.

Most technology education falls into one of two traps: it either stays completely abstract and theoretical (interesting but not useful) or it becomes a collection of commands to memorize without understanding why they work (useful in the short term, fragile in practice). CyberLab tries to occupy the space between — rigorous theory explained through practical application.

The six domains covered here aren't arbitrary. They form a complete stack. Electronics is the hardware. Computer science and informatics are the software and systems layer. Mathematics is the formal language. Cybersecurity is the discipline of protecting everything. And ethical hacking is the methodology for testing that protection. Each article connects back to this bigger picture.

This is a living project. New articles are added regularly. Projects are documented in detail. If you spot an error, disagree with an explanation, or want to suggest a topic — reach out. Good-faith feedback makes this better.

Essential toolkit

Tools of the Trade

The tools used across all six domains — from network analysis to hardware debugging to cryptography. Every professional tool here is either free, open-source, or has a free version sufficient for learning.

🔍
Wireshark
Network Analysis
The world's most widely used network protocol analyzer. Captures and inspects packets in real time. Essential for understanding what actually travels across a network — and for spotting anomalies in traffic.
🕷️
Burp Suite
Web Security
The standard tool for web application penetration testing. Intercepts HTTP/S requests, allows modification, runs automated scanners, and manages findings. The Community edition is free and extremely capable.
🗺️
Nmap
Reconnaissance
Network Mapper — scans networks for open ports and running services, identifies operating systems, and can run scripted detection of known vulnerabilities. The starting point of almost every engagement.
💻
Metasploit
Exploitation Framework
The most widely used exploitation framework in the world. Contains hundreds of tested exploit modules, payload generators, and post-exploitation tools. Understanding Metasploit is understanding how professional attackers operate.
🔐
Hashcat
Cryptanalysis
The world's fastest password recovery tool. Uses GPU acceleration to crack MD5, SHA-1, bcrypt, WPA2, and hundreds of other hash types. Essential for understanding why password storage matters.
🍳
CyberChef
Data Transformation
The "Swiss Army knife" of data manipulation — browser-based tool for encoding, decoding, encrypting, hashing, and transforming data. Invaluable in CTF challenges and day-to-day security analysis.
🔭
Ghidra
Reverse Engineering
NSA's open-source software reverse engineering framework. Disassembles compiled binaries into readable assembly and pseudo-C code. The free alternative to IDA Pro, used extensively in malware analysis.
🐛
GDB + pwndbg
Debugging & Exploitation
GNU Debugger with the pwndbg extension for binary exploitation. Step through assembly instructions, inspect memory, set breakpoints, and understand exactly what happens during a buffer overflow.
🔬
Volatility
Memory Forensics
Framework for analyzing RAM dumps from live or compromised systems. Extract running processes, network connections, registry keys, and even encryption keys from memory images — a forensics essential.
📡
Aircrack-ng
Wireless Security
Suite of tools for assessing WiFi network security. Packet capture, WEP and WPA/WPA2-PSK cracking, fake access point creation. Use only on networks you own or have explicit permission to test.
🐍
Python + Scapy
Scripting & Packet Crafting
Python is the scripting language of security. Scapy allows creating, sending, sniffing, and dissecting network packets at a low level — perfect for custom tooling and protocol research.
⚡
Arduino IDE + PlatformIO
Embedded Development
The development environment for Arduino microcontrollers and embedded systems. PlatformIO is the professional extension supporting dozens of boards, better dependency management, and VS Code integration.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: January 2025 · CyberLab · Hussein

Overview

CyberLab is a personal educational website. This policy explains what data we collect (very little), why, and what rights you have. Plain language, no legal theater.

Who We Are

CyberLab is operated by Hussein as a personal knowledge platform. Contact: cyberLab.student@proton.me. No phone number is provided.

What Data We Collect

CyberLab is designed to be privacy-respecting by default. We do not:

  • Use advertising networks or sell your data
  • Track you with cookies or persistent identifiers
  • Collect data beyond what you explicitly send us
  • Use invasive analytics platforms (no Google Analytics, no Meta Pixel)

The contact form on this site opens your own email client — no form data is transmitted to or stored by CyberLab's server. The message goes directly from your email client to our ProtonMail inbox.

Cookies

This website sets no cookies. Zero. Nothing is stored in your browser by CyberLab. Your preferences (like article filter choices) are held in JavaScript memory only and disappear when you close the tab.

Third-Party Services

We use Google Fonts to load typography (Syne, Space Mono, IBM Plex Sans). When fonts load, your browser sends a request to Google's servers, which receives your IP address. If you want to prevent this, use a browser extension like uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger — both block font CDN requests.

No other third-party services are embedded on this site. No social media widgets, no tracking pixels, no analytics SDKs.

Email Communication

When you contact cyberLab.student@proton.me, your email is processed by Proton AG, headquartered in Switzerland. Proton provides end-to-end encrypted email. Messages are retained only as long as needed to respond meaningfully. We do not share email correspondence with third parties.

Your Rights (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, you have the following rights under GDPR:

  • Right of access — Request a copy of data we hold about you
  • Right to erasure — Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to rectification — Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to object — Object to data processing
  • Right to portability — Receive your data in a structured format

To exercise any right, email cyberLab.student@proton.me with the subject line "GDPR Request." Hussein will respond personally.

Security

Security is literally what this site is about. HTTPS is enforced. Email is end-to-end encrypted. The contact form opens your own email client rather than submitting to a server — this reduces the attack surface to essentially zero on our side.

Children's Privacy

CyberLab does not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently received information from a minor, contact us immediately and it will be deleted.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated. The effective date at the top shows when it was last changed. Significant changes will be noted in the update log below.
